Abstract

From sparse radar signals
to complete human shapes

mmWave radar enables non-intrusive, privacy-preserving human sensing under all lighting and weather conditions, but its inherent sparsity (50–150 points per frame) fundamentally limits downstream analysis. Existing point cloud completion methods are designed for dense, rigid-object inputs and fail under the extreme sparsity and non-rigid topology of the human body.

We propose SKD-Net, a two-path framework that decouples structural alignment from shape completion. SkelKD learns cross-modal skeleton joint coordinates from radar observations via attention-based soft aggregation, resolving the spatial misalignment between depth-camera skeletons and radar space. A PE Encoder then compresses sparse points into skeleton-aligned latent tokens Z₀. A three-signal Condition Network — fusing skeleton GCN topology, skeleton-guided mmWave geometry, and action category — guides a Dynamic Transformer to denoise Z_T → Z₀, recovering complete, action-consistent human point clouds.

SKD-Net significantly outperforms both deterministic completion baselines (PCN, PoinTr, Snowflake, LAKe-Net) and generative baselines (LION, TIGER) on MM-Fi, while maintaining temporal consistency across frames.

Method

Two-path skeleton-guided architecture

Path ① establishes structural priors: SkelKD aligns skeleton joints to radar space, while PE Encoder and Auto-encoder E produce skeleton-aligned latent tokens and coarse geometry, respectively. Path ② performs conditional latent diffusion guided by these priors through a cross-attention Dynamic Transformer.

Figure 1. Overview of SKD-Net. Path ① (left): SkelKD, PE Encoder, Auto-encoder E, Condition Network. Path ② (right): Forward diffusion and Dynamic Transformer reverse denoising.

01

SkelKD

Cross-modal skeleton detector. Refines depth-camera joints using radar observations via PCN encoder and attention-based joint feature aggregation, resolving coordinate system misalignment.

02

PE Encoder

Compresses variable-length sparse point clouds into fixed 8×120 latent tokens Z₀ via skeleton-anchored cross-attention and VAE reparameterization. Shared decoder for reconstruction.

03

Condition Network

Fuses skeleton GCN tokens (17), coarse geometry tokens (128), and action category label into a unified 145-token K/V sequence for cross-attention conditioning.

04

Dynamic Transformer

Denoises Z_T → Z₀ through N cross-attention blocks conditioned on the unified condition tokens. Adaptive LayerNorm modulated jointly by time embedding and action category.
